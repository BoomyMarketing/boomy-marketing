What Is SEO Pricing Based On?

How We Price SEO for Canadian Businesses

SEO pricing is not one-size-fits-all. The investment required to achieve meaningful results depends on three primary factors: the competitiveness of your target keywords, the current state of your website (a site with significant technical debt requires more initial work), and the geographic scope of your campaign (local SEO in a smaller Canadian city requires less investment than national SEO for competitive terms).

Boomy Marketing conducts a free competitive analysis before providing any pricing. This analysis gives us a clear picture of what it will realistically take to move the needle for your specific business in your specific market. We then build a package around those requirements rather than offering generic tiers that may over or under-deliver on your actual needs.

Our pricing is fully transparent: what you see in your proposal is what you pay. No hidden fees for reporting, no extra charges for link building, no surprise invoices. Every deliverable is specified in your monthly retainer scope of work so you always know exactly what your investment covers.

The ROI of SEO

Why SEO Investment Pays Back

The most important thing to understand about SEO pricing is the return on investment over time. Unlike Google Ads where traffic stops when you stop paying, SEO builds rankings that deliver traffic continuously. A first-page ranking for a valuable keyword generates traffic and leads month after month, year after year, after the ranking is achieved.

A business investing $2,500 per month in SEO over 18 months ($45,000 total investment) that achieves rankings generating 100 qualified leads per month at a 20% close rate and $3,000 average customer value generates $60,000 per month in revenue from SEO. That is a return of 24x the monthly investment from the organic channel alone, and those rankings continue to deliver value beyond the initial investment period.

Compare this to Google Ads: to generate 100 leads per month at a typical Canadian B2B cost-per-lead of $150-300, you would spend $15,000-30,000 per month on an ongoing basis with no compounding benefit. The moment you reduce the budget, leads drop proportionally.

The businesses that struggle to justify SEO investment are those that evaluate it against a one-month payback window. SEO is a compounding asset. Evaluate it over a 24-month period and the ROI is almost always substantially better than any alternative digital marketing channel.

Understanding SEO Investment

Why SEO Costs What It Does: A Transparent Breakdown

The wide range of SEO pricing in Canada, from $300/month freelancers to $15,000/month agencies, creates genuine confusion for business owners evaluating their options. Understanding what drives SEO costs helps you make a decision based on value rather than price alone, and helps you identify when a low price reflects inadequate service rather than genuine efficiency.

Professional SEO requires significant time investment per client. A typical monthly SEO retainer for a Canadian business includes: 4-6 hours of technical monitoring and implementation, 8-12 hours of content research, writing, and optimisation, 6-8 hours of link building outreach and follow-up, 3-4 hours of reporting and strategy, and 2-3 hours of Google Business Profile management and citation building. At professional hourly rates, this represents a genuine cost that determines the minimum viable investment for effective SEO.

$300/month "SEO packages" cannot deliver this scope of work. These services typically involve automated reporting with no real analysis, bulk directory submissions using tools that Google devalued years ago, and templated content that creates more thin content risk than ranking benefit. Many Canadian businesses have spent 12-24 months with these services and achieved no meaningful results, then concluded that SEO does not work, when the actual conclusion should be that underfunded SEO does not work.

The return on investment equation for professional SEO is compelling when calculated correctly. A Canadian business ranking #1 for a keyword with 500 monthly searches receives approximately 300 monthly visitors from that one keyword. If their service converts at 3% and has an average value of $2,000, that single keyword ranking generates $18,000 in annual revenue. A comprehensive SEO campaign targeting 20-50 such keywords at various ranking positions typically generates 10-50x the cost of the SEO investment annually once rankings are established.

SEO ROI is also compounding in a way that other marketing channels are not. A Google Ads campaign stops generating traffic the moment you stop paying. An SEO investment that achieves strong rankings in year one continues generating traffic in year two, three, and beyond, with maintenance costs that are a fraction of the initial investment. This compounding nature makes SEO the highest long-term ROI marketing channel for most Canadian service businesses.

Boomy Marketing's pricing is structured around the scope of work genuinely required to achieve results in your specific market and competitive environment. We do not offer packages that look attractive but cannot deliver results. Every engagement begins with a competitive analysis that determines what investment level is needed to achieve meaningful rankings within a realistic timeframe in your market.
