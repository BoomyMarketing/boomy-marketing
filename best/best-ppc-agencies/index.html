What Do the Best PPC Companies and Agencies Do Differently?

Top PPC agencies spend the first 30 days diagnosing before they spend. They audit your conversion tracking to ensure every lead and sale is being measured accurately. They analyse your keyword landscape at the intent level, not just volume. They restructure campaigns around the customer journey — awareness, consideration, decision — rather than dumping all keywords into one campaign with a single bid strategy.

They also communicate clearly about what they can and cannot control. Click costs are determined by auction dynamics, not agency skill. Agency skill determines account structure, quality scores, ad relevance, landing page conversion rates, and bidding strategy — all of which compound over time.

How to Evaluate PPC Companies Before Signing a Contract

Ask for an audit of your existing account (or a competitor analysis if you are starting from scratch). Review the audit for depth — does it cover quality scores, impression share, search term waste, device performance, and audience overlap? Ask about their Google Ads certification status and minimum spend requirements. Verify that you own your ad account — never let an agency create an account in their own name with your payment method.

What PPC Results Should Canadian Businesses Expect?

For established Canadian businesses with well-structured accounts, realistic targets are: cost per lead improvements of 20–40% within 90 days of account restructuring, click-through rate improvements of 30–60% with new creative, and quality score improvements of 2–3 points across core keywords. ROAS targets depend heavily on your industry — typical Canadian benchmarks range from 2.5x for services businesses to 5–8x for e-commerce.

What Is the Difference Between Stopping and Pausing Google Ads?

Pausing Google Ads stops spend while preserving your campaign settings, quality scores, and historical performance data. Completely stopping Google Ads by letting your account go inactive causes quality score decay and loss of auction insights. If you need to reduce spend, pause campaigns rather than deleting or abandoning them. The best PPC agencies also maintain campaigns at minimal spend during slow seasons to preserve account history.

What Does PPC Management Cost in Canada?

Professional PPC management in Canada costs 10–20% of monthly ad spend with minimum management fees of CAD $1,500–$2,500/month. For a business spending $5,000/month on ads, expect $750–$1,000 in management fees. Full-service programs that include creative production, landing page optimisation, and CRM integration are priced at $2,000–$5,000/month. Be sceptical of agencies charging under $500/month — quality management cannot be delivered at this price point.
