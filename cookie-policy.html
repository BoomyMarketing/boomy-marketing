Cookie Policy

We believe in transparency about how we use cookies. This policy explains what cookies are, which ones we use, and how you can control them.

Last Updated: April 12, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide website operators with information about how their site is being used.

Cookies are not programs and cannot carry viruses or install malware on your device. Most web browsers accept cookies by default, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. Declining certain cookies may affect your ability to use some features of our website.

In addition to cookies, we may also use similar technologies such as web beacons (also known as "tracking pixels" or "clear GIFs"), local storage, and session storage. This policy applies to all such technologies collectively referred to as "cookies."

2. Who Sets Cookies on This Website?

Cookies on boomymarketing.com are set by:

  • Boomy Marketing Inc. (first-party cookies): Cookies we set directly to enable core website functionality and measure our website's performance
  • Third-party providers (third-party cookies): Cookies set by external services we use, such as Google Analytics, Google Ads, Meta Pixel, and LinkedIn Insight Tag. These third parties have their own privacy policies governing how they use cookie data

4. Your Cookie Choices and How to Manage Them

You have several options for controlling cookies:

4.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to manage cookies through the browser settings. You can usually find cookie settings in the "Privacy" or "Security" section of your browser. The links below provide instructions for the most popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome — Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox — Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Apple Safari — Preferences > Privacy
  • Microsoft Edge — Settings > Privacy, search, and services > Cookies and site permissions

Please note that deleting or disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website and your ability to use certain features.

4.2 Opt-Out of Analytics

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites by installing the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

4.3 Opt-Out of Interest-Based Advertising

You can opt out of interest-based advertising from participating companies in Canada through the Digital Advertising Alliance of Canada (DAAC) at youradchoices.ca. Note that opting out does not prevent you from seeing ads — it means the ads you see will be less tailored to your interests.

4.4 Platform-Specific Opt-Outs

  • Google: Manage ad personalization at adssettings.google.com
  • Meta (Facebook/Instagram): Manage ad preferences in your Facebook or Instagram account settings under "Ads"
  • LinkedIn: Opt out of LinkedIn's tracking by visiting your LinkedIn account's Privacy Settings, under "Data privacy" > "Ads"

5. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want to be tracked. Because there is currently no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, our website does not currently respond to DNT signals. We will revisit this policy as industry standards evolve.

6. Cookies and Children

If you believe a child has provided us with personal information through our website, please contact us at care@boomymarketing.com and we will take appropriate steps to address the situation.
