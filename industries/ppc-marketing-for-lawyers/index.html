What Digital Marketing Strategies Work Best for Canadian Law Firms?

Local SEO is the foundation of law firm digital marketing. Most legal searches in Canada are local — "family lawyer Toronto", "personal injury attorney Vancouver", "immigration lawyer Calgary". Ranking in the Google Local Pack for these searches delivers more phone calls than any other channel for most practice areas. Supporting pillars are Google Ads (for immediate visibility), content marketing (for long-tail SEO and trust building), and review management (Google rating directly impacts local pack visibility).

What Are the Law Society Rules for Law Firm Advertising in Canada?

Law Society advertising rules vary by province, but common requirements across Canada include: no misleading or deceptive advertising, no guarantees of results, disclosure of whether advertised services are available in English and French, and compliance with rules on testimonials (Ontario now allows client testimonials with certain restrictions). Boomy Marketing reviews all law firm advertising materials for Law Society compliance before publication.

How Does PPC Advertising Work for Law Firms?

Google Ads is highly effective for law firms because legal searches have extremely high commercial intent. Cost-per-click for competitive legal keywords in Canadian markets (personal injury, family law) ranges from $20–$80 CAD — expensive, but justified by the value of a new client matter. Effective law firm PPC requires tightly themed ad groups, landing pages dedicated to each practice area, click-to-call extensions, and proper Quality Score management to keep CPCs competitive.

How Do Canadian Law Firms Generate More Reviews?

Reviews are critical for local SEO in legal services. A law firm with 50+ Google reviews and a 4.5+ star rating dramatically outperforms competitors in local pack visibility. Ethical review generation involves asking satisfied clients personally (not in mass emails), making the process easy with a direct link to your Google review form, and responding professionally to every review — positive or negative. Most Law Societies permit review solicitation as long as it is not misleading.
