Marguerite Cumming bought the bungalow in November 2024 for the way the light came in. By February 2026, she was standing in the kitchen at 7 a.m., describing — accurately, almost forensically — exactly how it didn't. "The west wall is a furnace from 3 p.m. The south windows go straight glare. The bedroom won't get dark even at midnight in June." She poured coffee. "We need linen."

That call came on a Tuesday. By Friday a SunLeo designer was at the door with a fabric kit, a laser measure, and a leather binder of swatches. By the following Wednesday, Marguerite had a written quote (twenty-three windows, four rooms, three fabrics, layered drapery in the den). By the Friday after that, two installers were on her stoop at 7:50 a.m., booties already in hand.

"I asked them to make it feel like the house was finally on my side. Four mornings later, it was."

— Marguerite Cumming, Britannia SW

03.

Profile · 8 min

A founder's portrait

Iris Laframboise,
on slow craft.

Words: Eli Renwick · Photography: Lior Helm

She is forty-three years old, a mother of two, and the only person in Calgary I have ever heard pronounce the word "casement" with affection. We meet in the Inglewood shop on a Tuesday morning — Iris in a cream apron, the smell of fresh-cut linen, the radio low.

"We started in 2017 with one machine and a hundred dollars in fabric scraps from a Vancouver liquidation," she says, smoothing a panel with her palm. "We have five machines now. Same scraps, mostly."

In nine years SunLeo has dressed 3,427 Calgary homes. They've never advertised, never franchised, never let a single installation happen without Iris seeing the spec sheet. She still answers the email inbox. She still signs the orders.

04.

Field essay · 14 min

Chinook light, 4 p.m.

A short essay on the way Calgary weather moves through a south-facing kitchen — and the three quiet steps SunLeo takes, weekly, to keep up with it.

Plate 4.1 · A Bridgeland kitchen, 16:47, March 2026

When the Chinook arch sets up over the foothills in late February, Calgary's afternoon light goes warm fast — within forty minutes, the temperature in a west-facing room can climb seven degrees, and the sunbeam on a sectional turns the colour of a peach. By 4 p.m. the south-facing rooms have lost contrast: everything reads the same warm grey. This is when a window covering earns its keep.

We've measured the curve in roughly 1,200 Calgary homes. The pattern is consistent: south windows need 12–18% solar gain reduction, west windows need a layered solution — a sheer behind a heavier drape — and east windows, surprisingly, ask for the least intervention. A linen roller and a sheer is often enough.

Our atelier's process accounts for all three by default. Step one: swatches. Eight cut samples, in your home, in real Calgary light. Step two: in-home measure. A SunLeo designer at your door with a leather kit. Step three: install. Two of our people, drop cloths, slippers indoors. The whole arc, swatch to install, runs 5–8 business days. We do this every week.

12–16 windows in a 2,400 sq ft home lands between $4,200 and $11,400. Bedroom-only refresh starts at $890. Written, line-item quote within 24 h of your in-home visit.

5–8 business days for standard collections, 10–12 days for custom drapery. Install date confirmed the same day you approve the quote.

Lifetime, transferable to new homeowners. We've replaced 11-year-old cordless mechanisms free of charge.
