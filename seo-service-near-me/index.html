How Do Canadian Businesses Choose Between Local and National SEO Services?

If your business serves a specific geographic area — a city, a province, or a regional market — local SEO is your primary lever. Local SEO focuses on ranking in Google's map pack, building your Google Business Profile authority, and targeting city-specific keywords. If you sell nationally or operate an e-commerce business, national SEO (targeting broad, non-geographic keywords) becomes more important.

Most Canadian businesses need a combination. A Toronto law firm might want local dominance in the GTA plus national rankings for practice-area terms that attract clients from across Ontario. A Vancouver software company might need both local B2B visibility and national content authority. Boomy Marketing assesses the right balance for your specific business model and builds a proportionate campaign strategy.

What Are the Red Flags When Choosing an SEO Service Near You?

Several warning signs should prompt caution when evaluating any Canadian SEO service. First: guaranteed rankings. No ethical agency can guarantee specific search positions — Google's algorithm is proprietary and changes constantly. Second: extremely low pricing. Effective SEO requires substantial time investment; if an agency offers comprehensive SEO for $300 CAD/month, they are either automating everything with AI content or delivering almost nothing. Third: no reporting access. If you can't see your rankings, traffic data, and what work was done in any given month, transparency is absent.
