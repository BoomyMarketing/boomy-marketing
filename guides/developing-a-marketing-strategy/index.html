What Is Developing A Marketing Strategy?

Developing A Marketing Strategy is the structured plan that guides how a business communicates, positions itself, and allocates marketing resources to achieve measurable goals. For Canadian businesses, a strong marketing strategy is the difference between scattered tactics that drain budget and a coherent system that compounds results over time.

The Core Components of an Effective Marketing Strategy

Every effective marketing strategy includes: market and competitive analysis, clear target audience personas, positioning and messaging framework, channel selection and budget allocation, content and campaign calendar, and KPIs for measuring success. Boomy Marketing develops comprehensive strategies for Canadian businesses across all sectors.

How to Develop a Marketing Strategy in Canada

Start with data: analyze your current website traffic, conversion rates, customer acquisition costs, and competitive landscape. Define your Unique Value Proposition (UVP) clearly. Select 2–3 primary channels where your target audience is most active. Set 90-day, 6-month, and 12-month goals with specific metrics. Build your content and campaign plan around those goals.

Common Marketing Strategy Mistakes Canadian Businesses Make

The most common mistakes: no documented strategy (relying on intuition), trying to be on every channel simultaneously, ignoring organic channels in favour of paid-only approaches, not tracking attribution properly, and failing to iterate based on data. Boomy Marketing audits existing strategies and identifies these gaps before building a roadmap for improvement.

Digital vs. Traditional Marketing Strategy in Canada

Most Canadian SMBs today achieve the best ROI through digital channels: SEO, Google Ads, social media, email marketing, and content marketing. Traditional channels (print, radio, outdoor) remain relevant for certain local and brand-awareness goals. The right mix depends on your industry, target demographic, and budget.

How often should I update my marketing strategy?

Review your strategy quarterly and do a full refresh annually. Markets, algorithms, and consumer behaviour shift fast — a strategy written 18 months ago may be significantly out of date.

What is the difference between a marketing strategy and a marketing plan?

A strategy defines the why and what — your positioning, goals, and approach. A marketing plan defines the how — specific campaigns, timelines, budgets, and tactics. Both are necessary.
