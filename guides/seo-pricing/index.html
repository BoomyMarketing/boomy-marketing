SEO Pricing in Canada 2025 — What Does SEO Actually Cost?

Real CAD pricing for every tier of Canadian SEO service — from freelancers to full-service agencies. Written by Boomy Marketing, Canada's Growth Partner.

By Sarah Chen · December 1, 2025 · 8 min read
TL;DR

Canadian SEO services range from $500–1,500 CAD/month (freelancers) to $4,000–15,000+ CAD/month (full-service agencies for competitive markets). Most SMEs achieve strong results in the $1,500–3,500 CAD/month range. The cheapest option is almost never the best value — low-cost SEO risks Google penalties. Budget for at least 6 months to see meaningful organic growth.

What Is the Average Cost of SEO in Canada?

Canadian businesses investing in professional SEO services can expect to pay between $1,500 and $5,000 CAD per month for quality ongoing campaigns. This wide range reflects genuine differences in market competitiveness, scope of work, and agency expertise. A small business in Fredericton targeting local service searches has very different SEO requirements and costs than a Toronto law firm competing for high-value transactional keywords.

What Do You Get at Each SEO Price Point in Canada?

The quality and quantity of deliverables increases substantially with budget. At the $500–1,500 CAD/month freelancer tier, you typically receive basic keyword research, a limited number of on-page optimisations, and perhaps 1–2 blog posts per month. Backlink building is usually absent or minimal. Reporting is often a simple monthly ranking check rather than a comprehensive performance analysis.

At the $1,500–3,500 CAD/month small agency tier — which is Boomy Marketing's entry point — you receive a dedicated account manager, monthly technical audits and fixes, 3–4 content pieces per month, active citation building, Google Business Profile management, and link outreach. This level of service is appropriate for most Canadian SMEs and typically produces meaningful organic traffic growth within 4–6 months.

Enterprise-level campaigns at $7,500–15,000+ CAD/month involve full content teams, aggressive link acquisition programmes, technical development resources, and multi-location or multi-language campaign management. These are appropriate for businesses with substantial organic revenue potential that justifies significant investment.

Why Do Canadian SEO Prices Vary So Much Between Agencies?

The gap between a $700 CAD/month SEO provider and a $3,500 CAD/month agency reflects genuine differences in team size, content quality, link-building approach, and strategic depth. Many low-cost providers rely heavily on automated tools, templated content, and link networks that violate Google's guidelines. These tactics can produce short-term ranking improvements followed by severe penalties that take months to recover from.

Reputable Canadian agencies like Boomy Marketing invest in Canadian writers who understand local market context, ethical link acquisition through genuine outreach, and technical expertise that addresses Core Web Vitals, mobile optimisation, and structured data — all of which Google weighs in its 2025 ranking algorithm. This investment is reflected in pricing but justified by sustainable, compounding results.

Is SEO or Paid Ads a Better Investment for Canadian Businesses?

This is one of the most common questions Boomy Marketing receives, and the honest answer is: it depends on your timeline and business model. Paid ads (Google Ads, Meta Ads) produce immediate traffic but cost $8–25+ CAD per click in competitive Canadian markets, and traffic stops when you stop spending. SEO requires 3–6 months of investment before meaningful organic traffic arrives, but compounds over time into an asset that generates leads at near-zero marginal cost.

The most economically efficient approach for most Canadian businesses is to use paid ads for short-term lead generation while investing in SEO as a long-term asset. After 12–18 months, organic rankings typically replace a significant portion of paid spend, dramatically reducing overall customer acquisition costs. According to HubSpot, inbound leads (primarily organic search) cost 61% less than outbound leads. Search Engine Land reports that organic search drives over 50% of all digital traffic — making SEO the single most important long-term marketing investment for most businesses.

How Should You Budget for SEO in Canada?

Boomy Marketing recommends that Canadian businesses budget for a minimum of 6 months of SEO investment before evaluating ROI, with 12 months being the more realistic horizon for competitive markets. This is not a limitation of good SEO — it is simply how Google's ranking algorithm works. Sites earn authority and ranking through consistent quality signals over time, not through a single month of activity.

For budgeting purposes, calculate the lifetime value of a new customer in your business and the current cost of acquiring one through paid channels. If an organic search lead costs 50% less to acquire and converts at a similar rate, your SEO break-even point becomes clear. Most Canadian businesses at the $1,500–3,500 CAD/month tier achieve positive ROI within 8–12 months.
