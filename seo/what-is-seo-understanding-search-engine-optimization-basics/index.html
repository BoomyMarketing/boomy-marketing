SEO Explained

What Is SEO? Understanding Search Engine Optimisation Basics

SEO is the backbone of long-term digital growth. This overview explains what it is, how it works, and why every Canadian business needs it.

Published 2023-09-28Boomy Marketing

Defining SEO in the Modern Search Era

Search Engine Optimisation (SEO) is the discipline of improving a website visibility in organic search results. The goal is to appear as high as possible when potential customers search for products, services, or information related to your business. In 2025 and beyond, SEO extends beyond traditional Google rankings to encompass visibility in AI Overviews, Bing search, voice search, and emerging AI platforms like Perplexity and ChatGPT search. Businesses optimised for search today are positioning themselves for the full spectrum of organic discovery.

How Search Engines Work: Three Stages

Crawling: Search engine bots visit websites by following links, discovering new pages and revisiting existing ones. If a page cannot be crawled it cannot be indexed or ranked. Indexing: After crawling, search engines process and store page content in their index. Only indexed pages can appear in search results. Ranking: When a user searches, the engine retrieves relevant indexed pages and ranks them using hundreds of signals. The pages Google considers most relevant, authoritative, and user-friendly appear at the top.

What Changed in SEO: The Evolution

In the early days, stuffing keywords into a page was enough to rank. Today, Google algorithm understands natural language, assesses content quality, evaluates user experience, and detects manipulation. This evolution benefits legitimate businesses: genuine quality is now rewarded. The most significant recent changes include the Helpful Content Update (prioritising people-first content), Core Web Vitals becoming ranking signals, and AI Overviews changing how some informational queries are answered.

Measuring SEO Success: Visibility to Revenue

SEO success is measured through a hierarchy: Rankings (where do you appear for target keywords?), Organic Impressions (how often do your pages appear?), Organic Click-Through Rate (what percentage click your result?), Organic Traffic (how many visitors arrive via search?), Organic Conversion Rate (what percentage take a desired action?), and Revenue from Organic (what revenue is attributed to organic search?). FAQ

Common Questions

What is the difference between SEO and content marketing?

SEO tells you what your audience searches for and optimises content to rank. Content marketing creates valuable articles and guides that attract links and build authority. The most effective strategies use both together.

How does Google AI Overview affect SEO?

AI Overviews show AI-generated answers that can reduce clicks for informational queries. However being cited in AI Overviews is itself an SEO opportunity. Optimising for E-E-A-T and structured data helps your content get cited.

What is domain authority?

A Moz metric predicting how well a site will rank based on its backlink profile. Scored 0-100. Improve it by earning high-quality backlinks from authoritative websites over time.

Can SEO work for B2B businesses?

Absolutely. B2B buyers use search to research vendors. B2B SEO focuses on longer specific keywords reflecting the research phase, thought leadership content, and content that supports the sales process.

What is local vs national SEO?

Local SEO targets geographic searches and optimises for Google Maps. National SEO targets broader keywords across an entire country. Most small businesses should prioritise local SEO first.
