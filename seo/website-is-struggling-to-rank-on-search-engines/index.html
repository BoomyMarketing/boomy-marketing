SEO Troubleshooting

Why Your Website Is Struggling to Rank on Search Engines

If your website is not showing up in Google despite your best efforts, here are the most common reasons and how to fix them.

Published 2023-09-28Boomy Marketing

1. Technical Indexing Issues

Before Google can rank your pages, it needs to find and index them. Many websites unknowingly block Google through misconfigured robots.txt files, accidental noindex tags, or crawl errors. Check Google Search Console under Coverage to see which pages are indexed and which are excluded. A noindex tag on your homepage means it will never rank, regardless of how good the content is.

2. Content Does Not Match Search Intent

Even if your page is indexed, it will not rank if the content does not match what searchers are looking for. Google categorises searches by intent: informational, navigational, commercial, and transactional. You need different pages for different intents: guides for informational searches, service pages for transactional searches. A services page will not rank for a how-to query and a blog post will not rank for a commercial intent query.

3. Your Domain Has Too Little Authority

New websites almost always struggle to rank for competitive terms because they have no domain authority. Google uses backlinks as one of its most important ranking signals. Building authority takes time. Start with business directory listings, ask satisfied customers to link to you, reach out to local news outlets with newsworthy stories, and publish content that earns natural links. Domain authority compounds: the longer you build, the easier it becomes.

4. Your Pages Are Too Slow

Page speed is a direct Google ranking factor. Core Web Vitals measure real-world user experience and pages that score poorly are deprioritised in rankings. Common speed killers: unoptimised images, excessive JavaScript, no caching, and cheap shared hosting. Google PageSpeed Insights gives you a free detailed report on exactly what to fix.

5. Targeting Keywords That Are Too Competitive

Many businesses target high-volume highly competitive keywords from day one and wonder why they do not rank. New sites have no chance against established competitors for those terms. Start with long-tail keywords: more specific, lower-competition terms that still attract relevant traffic. Rank for those first, then compete for broader terms as your domain strength grows.

FAQ

Common Questions

How do I check if my website is indexed?

Type site:yourwebsite.com into Google. If pages appear they are indexed. Use Google Search Console for a complete view including indexing errors.

How long before expecting rankings?

For new websites with no authority, 6-12 months for competitive terms. Technical fixes on existing sites show results in 4-8 weeks.

Can I recover from a Google penalty?

Yes but it takes time. Manual penalties require fixing the issue and submitting a reconsideration request. Algorithmic penalties resolve as you improve your site.

Why did my rankings suddenly drop?

Usually caused by a Google algorithm update, technical issue, competitor improvement, or penalty. Check Search Console for manual actions.
