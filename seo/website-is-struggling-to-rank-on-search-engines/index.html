SEO Troubleshooting

Why Your Website Is Struggling to Rank on Search Engines

If your website is not showing up in Google despite your best efforts, one or more of these five problems is the likely cause. Each is fixable with the right approach.

1. Technical Indexing Issues Are Blocking Your Visibility

Before Google can rank your pages, it needs to find them, crawl their content, and add them to its index. Many websites unknowingly block this process through misconfigured robots.txt files, accidental noindex meta tags, JavaScript rendering issues that prevent Google from seeing page content, or crawl errors that prevent Googlebot from accessing pages.

Check Google Search Console under the Index Coverage report to see which pages are indexed and which are excluded, and why. Common exclusion reasons include: 'Crawled, currently not indexed' (Google saw the page but chose not to index it, usually due to thin or duplicate content), 'Excluded by noindex tag' (an accidental noindex directive is blocking the page), and 'Redirect error' (broken redirect chains preventing crawling).

A noindex tag on an important page is catastrophic for SEO and surprisingly common. We have audited dozens of Canadian business websites where a single misconfigured setting blocked entire sections from Google. One Toronto law firm we worked with had their entire services section marked noindex due to a plugin configuration error. They had essentially been invisible in Google for nine months before Boomy Marketing identified the issue. Three weeks after the fix, their services pages began appearing in search results and their organic lead volume increased substantially.

JavaScript-heavy websites present a specific challenge. Google can render JavaScript but does so on a second crawl pass that may lag weeks behind the initial crawl. If your website's content is loaded entirely by JavaScript and Google cannot see it in the initial HTML response, your pages may have much weaker rankings than their content quality deserves. Server-side rendering or static HTML alternatives solve this problem.

2. Your Content Does Not Match What Searchers Are Looking For

Even if your page is perfectly indexed, it will not rank if the content format or type does not match the dominant search intent for your target keyword. Google categorises searches by intent and returns different result types for different intent categories. For informational queries like 'how to do SEO,' Google shows articles and guides. For commercial queries like 'best SEO agency Toronto,' it shows review sites, agency homepages, and comparison content. For transactional queries like 'hire SEO agency Toronto,' it shows direct service provider pages.

If you are trying to rank a blog post for a commercial query or a service page for an informational query, you are fighting the algorithm regardless of your content quality. The solution is to study the actual search results for your target keyword and note the dominant content type, format, and depth. If the first page shows 1,500-word guides, that is what Google considers best for that query. Create content that matches or exceeds that standard in the same format.

Search intent mismatch is one of the most common and most correctable ranking problems we diagnose at Boomy Marketing. We audit the top 10 results for every target keyword before creating or optimising content, ensuring every piece of content we produce is aligned with what Google and searchers expect.

3. Your Domain Has Insufficient Authority

New and young websites almost always struggle to rank for competitive terms because they have accumulated little domain authority. Google uses backlinks as one of its primary authority signals: a link from a trusted website is a vote of confidence that tells Google your content is worth ranking. Without these signals, even excellent content can sit on page three or four indefinitely while older, more authoritative sites rank above it with less comprehensive content.

Building authority takes time. The businesses that are ranking at the top of competitive Canadian search results today spent years earning the backlinks that put them there. This is not a reason to avoid SEO but it is a reason to start as soon as possible. Every month of authority-building investment compounds the advantage you hold over future competitors who start later.

The fastest legitimate authority-building tactics for Canadian businesses: local business directory listings build foundational citation authority in weeks. Joining your local Chamber of Commerce or Business Improvement Association typically includes a listing and link. Getting coverage in local news (even small community papers) builds high-quality Canadian links. Creating genuinely useful resources, tools, or original research that people in your industry naturally want to link to is the highest-value long-term tactic.

4. Your Pages Are Too Slow on Mobile

Page speed is a direct Google ranking factor through Core Web Vitals. Google measures LCP (Largest Contentful Paint, target under 2.5 seconds), CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift, target under 0.1), and INP (Interaction to Next Paint, target under 200ms). Pages that fail these thresholds are explicitly deprioritised in rankings, particularly for mobile searches which account for the majority of Google queries.

Google PageSpeed Insights gives you a free, detailed report on exactly what is causing performance problems on your pages. The most common issues are: unoptimised images (the largest single contributor to slow pages), render-blocking JavaScript loaded in the document head, excessive third-party scripts, and no browser caching configured. Fixing these issues often moves mobile scores from 20-40 to 70-90, which can produce measurable ranking improvements within 30-60 days.

5. You Are Targeting Keywords That Are Too Competitive

Many businesses target high-volume, highly competitive keywords from day one and wonder why they never rank. A new or young domain has virtually no chance of ranking for 'digital marketing agency' against Clutch.co, HubSpot, and thousands of established agencies with years of authority building behind them. This is not a failure of your SEO approach. It is simply the wrong keyword for your current authority level.

The solution is keyword strategy based on your current authority. Start with long-tail keywords: more specific, lower-competition terms that still attract relevant, qualified traffic. A digital marketing agency in Hamilton should target 'digital marketing agency Hamilton' before trying to compete for 'digital marketing agency Canada.' Win the local keywords first, build authority and trust signals, then expand to broader competitive terms as your domain strength grows. Boomy Marketing builds keyword roadmaps for every client that sequence keyword targets by competition and authority requirements, ensuring you win achievable rankings quickly while building toward more competitive terms over 12-24 months.

FAQ

Common Questions

How do I check if my website is indexed in Google?

Type site:yourwebsite.com into Google search. If pages appear in the results, they are indexed. For a complete picture including indexing errors and excluded pages, use Google Search Console's Coverage report, which shows every URL Google has encountered on your site and its indexing status.

Why did my Google rankings suddenly drop?

Sudden ranking drops usually result from one of four causes: a Google algorithm update that changed how your site is evaluated; a technical issue introduced by a site update (such as an accidental noindex tag or robots.txt change blocking crawlers); a significant increase in competitor quality; or a manual penalty from Google for guideline violations. Check Search Console for manual actions first, then compare your change history to algorithm update dates.

How long before I can expect to rank in Google?

For brand new websites with no existing authority, 6-12 months is a realistic timeline for competitive local keywords. Technical and on-page fixes to an existing site can produce visible improvements in 4-8 weeks. Boomy Marketing conducts a thorough competitive analysis for every client to set realistic, market-specific ranking timelines rather than generic estimates.

Can a Google penalty permanently destroy rankings?

Permanent penalties are rare and typically reserved for severe, repeated violations. Most penalties are reversible. Manual penalties require fixing the specified issue and submitting a reconsideration request through Search Console. Algorithmic penalties from content quality updates resolve over time as you improve your site's quality signals. Recovery timelines range from weeks for minor issues to 6-12 months for sites with extensive problems.

Should I hire an SEO agency or do it myself?

For businesses competing in moderately to highly competitive Canadian markets, a professional SEO agency like Boomy Marketing will consistently deliver faster, more reliable results than DIY approaches. The cost is offset by the revenue generated from improved rankings. For businesses in low-competition local markets with time to invest in learning, DIY SEO for the foundational tasks is feasible.
